## The Open-Source Revolution in Life Science

This repository contains the source materials for the presentation **The Open-Source Revolution in Life Sciences** by Eric Nantz at the 2025 Grand Valley State University [Statistics Career Day](https://www.gvsu.edu/stat/statistics-career-day-2025-123.htm) event on November 17, 2025. You can view the web version of the presentation slides at [r-podcast.github.io/gvsu-stats-day](https://r-podcast.github.io/gvsu-stats-day). A PDF version of the slides (missing the interactive elements) can be downloaded [here](https://rpodcast-misc.us-ord-1.linodeobjects.com/Open-Source%20Revolution%20in%20Life%20Sciences.pdf).

This presentation was authored with the [Quarto](https://quarto.org) scientific publishing system and managed with the [Nix](https://nixos.org/) package management framework assisted with the [`{rix}`](https://docs.ropensci.org/rix/) R package.

## Speaker Biography

Eric Nantz is a senior director within the Statistical Innovation Center at Eli Lilly and Company. Eric has over 16 years of experience across multiple roles as a clinical trial statistician and statistical software engineer creating analytical pipelines and capabilities of advanced statistical methodologies for clinical design used in multiple phases of development. In addition to his role at Eli Lilly, Eric is a founding member and co-organizer of the R/Pharma organization, as well as member of the R-Consortium working groups addressing the use of R and other open-source technology in clinical trial submissions. Outside of his day job, Eric is passionate about connecting with and showcasing the brilliant R community with his multiple podcasts dedicated to R and Data Science.

Eric is a proud alum of the Statistics program at Grand Valley State University (1998-2003) where he graduated with a major in Statistics and minor in Computer Science. Afterwards, Eric earned a Masters degree and Ph.D. in Statistics from Western Michigan University (2003-2009).
